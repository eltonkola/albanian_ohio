📊 Columbus Metro Summary

The Columbus metropolitan area is home to a growing Albanian and Kosovar community, largely established through post-1990s migration waves — particularly the Kosovo War refugee resettlement of 1998-1999. Columbus was a major resettlement city via CRIS Ohio (Community Refugee & Immigration Services), and the community has continued growing through family reunification and new arrivals.

The PUMS data shown here covers the Columbus metro PUMAs: Franklin County (core) plus adjacent Delaware/Fairfield/Licking area PUMAs. With ~376 people in the broad estimate from only 13 unweighted PUMS records, these numbers carry higher margins of error than the statewide data. They should be interpreted as directional indicators rather than precise counts.

Small Sample Caveat
PUMS microdata for small populations in specific geographies can have wide confidence intervals. The Columbus metro subset draws from 13 unweighted records (each representing ~29 people on average). The ACS B04006 published table reports 151 Albanian-ancestry individuals in Franklin County. Cross-referencing with birthplace data yields a broader estimate of ~376. For the statewide picture with larger sample sizes, see the Ohio statewide page.

✈️ Migration Timeline

The Columbus Albanian community is distinctly newer than Cleveland's. The year-of-entry data shows arrivals concentrated in the 2000s and 2010s, reflecting the post-Kosovo War resettlement wave and subsequent family reunification. Unlike Cleveland (which has roots in the early 1900s), Columbus's Albanian community is overwhelmingly first-generation.

🔧 Data & Methods

How This Page Was Generated

This page uses the same PUMS microdata processing pipeline as the statewide page, but filtered to Columbus metro area PUMAs. The filtering works as follows:

AreaPUMA PrefixCounties Covered
Core034xxFranklin County (Columbus proper)
Metro ring037xxDelaware / Union County area
Metro ring038xxFairfield / Licking County area

All population estimates are PWGTP-weighted. The same tiered Albanian filtering is used: Albanian ancestry (ANC1P/ANC2P=100) OR born in Albania/Kosovo (POBP 100/167) as core, plus Montenegro/N.Macedonia/Serbia/Yugoslavia born only if also Albanian ancestry.

Limitations
PUMA boundaries don't perfectly align with the official Columbus MSA definition. Some PUMAs in the 037xx and 038xx ranges may extend beyond the Columbus metro. Additionally, with only 13 unweighted PUMS records, individual demographic breakdowns have wide confidence intervals. The ACS B04006 county table provides a more precise ancestry count (151 for Franklin County) but doesn't allow cross-tabulation with birthplace or other variables.
